Base Stealing Intelligence, Spring 2018 Sabermetrics

Jake Mitchell

Welcome to my website for calculating a player's base stealing intelligence! This is an exercise in seeing how well we can quantify how smart a given player is with regards to stealing bases. Luckily with the new statcast data we are able to get sprint speeds for most of the players in the MLB for 2017. There is a definite correlation between a player's top speed and how well they perform on the bases. While there is a correlation, there is a ton of noise in the data, that I believe is due to how well a player uses their athletic ability. This website will be a work in progress, as I am very new to web development. I will start with putting static things up here, and will steadily move towards a fully interactive website. All of the backend statistics and plotting is complete. All i need to do is learn how in the world html css and python all work together... hmmmm

Here is the basis of what I will be working on. This is a plot of 436 player's weighted Stolen Bases from the 2017 season, against their top speed. I have fit a series of polynomials to the data to see if I can come up with a trend line that could predict with relative accuracy what an average players wSB would be given their top speed. While they do not fit the data insanely well, eyeballing it says they do a decent job of hitting the middle of the data which is exactly what we want. As far as mapping everything well I have found that a 5th degree polynomial works the best.

I plan on estimating a players Base Stealing Intelligence Runs (BSIR) by taking a player's wSB difference from this league trend line. The trend line gives us what kind of a runs a player is expected to produce given their physical ability represented by their maximum speed. A players difference from this line should give us the number of runs they produced by stealing bases well, rather than just by being fast.

Benefits of BSIR

  • Quantifies something new and unique
  • Centered around 0
  • In units of runs
  • Is relative to league average

Downsides of BSIR

  • Dependant on what polynomial chosen
  • Assumes that the difference from the predicted value based on speed is all due to their base running intelligence

An interesting feature we can see here is that as player's speed increases, the variancein their base stealing intelligence also goes up. This is likely due to the fact that I am holding them to a higher standard, meaning those who are performing near the acutal league average that doesn't account for their speed, will be marked very low in BSIR. Those who are marked as having very high BSIR are likely those who train to be great at base stealing, most of whom have the speed to begin with.
